# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Wedding guests invited to Ellie & Chris's wedding on 10 July 2027 at Hobbit Hill, Clitheroe. They are friends and family who received a physical save-the-date card and are now visiting the site to RSVP and get details. They use this once or twice on a phone or desktop; curiosity and warmth, not task efficiency, drive their experience.

## Product Purpose

A personal wedding website that replaces and extends the paper save-the-date. It collects RSVPs, shares the day's details, and makes guests feel genuinely welcomed. Success is a guest who smiles, RSVPs immediately, and feels like they already know the day ahead.

## Brand Personality

Warm, romantic, unhurried. Like a beautifully printed card come to life. The design draws directly from the couple's real save-the-date: soft sage-green paper, warm-white serif type, a delicate gold-flecked gypsophila arch, calligraphic accents. British, personal, gently witty — never corporate, never template.

Anti-references: generic wedding site templates (The Knot, Zola house styles), SaaS-cream landing pages, bouncy app animations, aggressively typographic editorial-magazine aesthetics.

## Design Principles

1. **The card, brought to life.** Every design decision should feel like a natural extension of the physical save-the-date — not a website that happens to be about a wedding.
2. **Warmth through restraint.** Elegance from what is left out. No noise, no decoration for decoration's sake.
3. **Unhurried.** Motion should breathe, not bounce. Guests are not in a hurry; the pace of the site should match the pace of an afternoon in the Ribble Valley.
4. **Personal, not performative.** Copy and interactions speak directly to this couple's story, not to a generic "wedding" category.
5. **One flowing experience.** After the RSVP, everything the guest needs unfolds on a single scroll — no menus, no navigation, no decisions about where to go next.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA. Respect `prefers-reduced-motion` — all animations must have a crossfade or instant fallback. Sufficient color contrast on all text.
